The Vision

Heritage as the foundation, tourism as the vehicle

Melghat Vikas Foundation is building a pilgrimage corridor that connects Adivasi communities across Madhya Pradesh, Maharashtra, Chhattisgarh and beyond. Three rivers, dense forest, and centuries of tribal history become the infrastructure for something new: a place people travel to, not past.

The goal isn't a monument for outsiders to admire — it's an economy tribal communities can own, staffed by tribal hands, telling tribal stories.

01 — PRIMARY LANDMARK
Primary Landmark

Sant Jatu Baba Museum & Temple Complex

A grand architectural complex celebrating tribal freedom fighters and cultural heritage, built to hold both pilgrims and scholars.

  • Tribal Heritage & Freedom gallery
  • Interactive cultural exhibits
  • Capacity for 5,000+ visitors daily
Spiritual Symbol

100‑Foot Birsa Munda Statue

A towering tribute to the legendary Adivasi freedom fighter, overlooking the sacred valley as a beacon of tribal pride.

  • Architectural marvel with museum base
  • Panoramic valley views
  • Sacred gathering space
