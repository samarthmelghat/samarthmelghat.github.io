Discover Melghat

A land where
history breathes
in every stone

Nestled in the Satpura ranges of Amravati district, Melghat is a living tapestry of tribal traditions, medieval fortifications, and pristine forests. The Samarth Melghat initiative bridges these worlds — inviting travellers to walk with the Korku people, trace ancient pilgrimage paths, and witness wildlife in its truest habitat.

From the misty heights of Chikhaldara to the sacred groves of the Devrais, every trail tells a story that's waited centuries to be heard.

Tribal Heritage

Guardians of the Forest

Indigenous communities preserving their way of life for millennia — in harmony with the land.

Korku Tribe

The largest tribal community, known for deep forest connections and a unique dialect.

Art & Craft

Bamboo craft, wall paintings, and traditional jewelry carrying generations of expression.

Music & Dance

Rhythmic dhol beats, tandi dances, and folk songs echoing through Satpura valleys.

Forest Wisdom

Ancient knowledge of medicinal plants and sustainable living passed down orally.

Getting There

How to Reach

By Air

Nearest: Amravati (100 km) or Nagpur (230 km). Taxis & buses available from both.

By Train

Amravati station (90 km) is well-connected. MSRTC buses run regularly to Chikhaldara.

By Road

Chikhaldara is 76 km from Amravati via Dharni. Scenic ghat roads through dense forest.
